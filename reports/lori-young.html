Your Point of View
The Uncomfortable Truth
Generalists are dying — not because their expertise isn't real, but because AI can now do "a little bit of everything" for free, which means "I can help with whatever you need" has stopped being a sellable position. If your offers speak to a vague, big problem instead of one sharp, specific one, you'll keep losing — to AI, or to the specialist down the street — no matter how good your work actually is.
Your Differentiation
Why Only You
Most people in this space pick one lane — messaging, or mindset, or funnels — and stay there. You work across the entire offer lifecycle, because you've lived the cost of not doing that: eight years as a generalist offering coaching, marketing, launches, branding, websites and funnels, until the market got crowded and you sounded like everyone else. You rebuilt around the OfferMojo™ Method, then built an AI-powered Squad that walks clients through the same sequence you use 1:1, so the depth doesn't disappear when the budget is smaller. You're not teaching a framework you read about — you're teaching the reinvention you had to survive.
Your Zone of Genius
What You Own
Your zone of genius is the intersection of structure and creativity — you can hear someone's scattered story about everything they do, and hand back one sharp sentence that names what they're actually known for. Beyond the positioning doc or messaging guide, what you really hand clients is the language they'll use in every sales call, post and pitch from that point forward — so they stop starting from zero.

What to Do This Month

Your Quick Wins

Simple actions you can take this month to build real momentum for your brand.

Week 1
Choose Your One Signature Line
Your strongest differentiator — that you work across the entire offer lifecycle, not one slice of it — currently shows up in three different forms across your story, your homepage, and your intake answers. Pick one version ("Most coaches focus on one piece. I work across the full system.") and use it as your bio opener, your call opener, and your content sign-off for the next 30 days.
Week 1
Meet Her Before the "Authority" Moment
Your homepage opens with the destination — "Stop Blending In. Claim Your Expertise." Add one section above it that names where she actually is right now, using the Hook from this Blueprint: "You launched it. You're proud of it. So why isn't it selling?" Let her recognize herself before you talk about where she's headed.
Week 2
Pick the Front Door
Your ideal-client answer names two people: the founder launching their first offer, and the established expert pivoting. Choose one to lead with in your bio and discovery-call script for the next month — likely the pivoting expert, since your case studies (Andrea, Ilana, Bradley) all describe that journey. The other audience will still recognize themselves; they just won't be first.
Week 3
Codify the Psychographic Profile
The impostor syndrome, the "is it me or the brand" fear, the comparison to others who "seem to have it figured out" — this language is gold, and right now it lives mostly in your head and your client conversations. Add two or three of these exact phrases to your Offer X-Ray intake questions, so prospects see themselves reflected before they ever talk to you.
Week 4
Name the Pricing Pattern
Every client story in your answers follows the same arc: undercharging, a pricing pushback, and a client who "didn't even flinch" once they raised their price. This pattern is repeatable and provable — turn it into a short video, blog post, or lead magnet ("Why You're Underpricing — And What Changes When Your Offer Catches Up"). It's the deepest proof of your method, and right now it's buried inside testimonials.
