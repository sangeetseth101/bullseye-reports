What to Do This Month

Your Quick Wins

Your quick wins — simple actions you can take this month to build real momentum for your brand.

Week 1
Make the Doctor the Hero of Your Homepage
Right now skinsutra.com leads with the products. Rewrite your homepage hero to lead with you: "Physician-Designed Skincare for the Midlife Transition — by Dr. Suzanne Gokel." One line. Your medical credibility is your biggest selling point and it is currently invisible above the fold.
Week 1
Name Your Method
Your products already use a RE- naming system — REBALANCE, RESIST, REGENERATE, REPLENISH, REVITALIZE. That is not just a product range. That is a branded philosophy. Give it a name — "The RE- Method" or "The RE- System" — and start using it in your copy so people understand they are buying into a methodology, not just individual products.
Week 2
Get Three Written Testimonials That Tell the Full Story
Your best clients know that working with you goes beyond the skincare. Ask three of them to write two or three sentences about what changed for them — not just physically but how they felt about themselves. That language is the most powerful copy you will ever have, and right now it does not exist on your website.
Week 3
Build the Menopausal Skin Content Pillar
You are one of the only people qualified to talk about what menopause actually does to skin from a medical perspective — and your audience is desperate for this information from someone they can trust. Write four posts on this topic this month. You will own the most specific and credible corner of your niche before anyone else gets there.
Week 4
Package Your In-Person Conversion Story for Digital
More than 90% of your sales happen when someone meets you in person. That is not a coincidence — it means when people experience your expertise, warmth, and personalization together, they buy. Write one page that recreates that experience: why people choose Skin Sutra after meeting Dr. Suzanne. Then put it on your website. That page closes the trust gap for everyone who finds you online first.
