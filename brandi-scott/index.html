What to Do This Week

Your Quick Wins

Actionable. Not aspirational. Each tied directly to your open pillar gaps.

Day 1
Rewrite Your Website Headline
"Communication and Leadership Consultant for Higher Education and Nonprofits" describes a category. It does not speak to anyone's fear. Try: "I help college campus leaders hold their teams together through change — without losing the people doing the work." One line. The biggest single-day leverage you have right now.
Day 2-3
Write Your Origin Story
Your path as a first-generation high school graduate who built a career and a PhD in higher education is not a footnote. It is the thing that makes you impossible to replicate. Write three paragraphs. Start with the moment you felt like you did not belong. End with: "That is why I see it so fast when a leader I work with feels the same way." This converts trust faster than any credential on your bio.
This Week
Name Your Method
You have a layered approach: individual coaching for the leader, targeted professional development for the team, pre/mid/post assessment, and a final roadmap. That deserves a name. Even a working title like the Layered Leadership System makes it ownable. It shows up in proposals, on your website, and in content. Look at your answers to Q5 and Q7. The framework is already there.
30 Days
Publish From Pillar 4
Nobody else in higher ed leadership consulting is talking about imposter syndrome from a place of lived first-gen experience AND a PhD in higher education leadership. That combination is yours alone. Write one piece using Pillar 4 — The First-Gen Leader's Edge. This pillar alone will differentiate you faster than any credential update. It is where your deepest authority lives.
